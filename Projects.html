Project 1 - Death

This is one of the peices that i worked on when it came to the exam period in my A Level Photography Course. The theme was 'Endings' and i looked into the biblical reference of 'The Four Horsemen Of the Apocalypse', this is the passage that states there are four ways the world will end and one of these ways and the horsemen is named Death. To portray this I am using nature's death as the main focal point, so I have been using the death of roses as a way to show the stages to plant death.
I originally looked at all the horsemen until I futher looked at 'Death' and saw an opportunity to hone in my skills on this section of my wider idea.

I looked into the works of a photographer named Keith Dotson, one of his focal points was dead flowers, in black and white so this was part of my inspiration to look more into dead and withering flowers, this also linked in with one of the horsemen on the bigger picture. This may only be one of them but it will show the idea when explained to the examiner through my own photographs. Upon further research I looked into Andrés Rozo Samer who also photographed dead flowers and leaves, making them curl and displaying them in unique and artistic ways. I then replicated the work focussing closely on aspects of their work like the subject and how certain areas are focussed on within their own work.

Keith Dotson



Keith Dotson is a contemporary photographer living in Nashville, Tennessee, who creates art for a global audience. He specialises in black and white photography and his favourite subjects include landscapes, cityscapes and abstractions from nature.

With photography, Keith Dotson presents straight-forward black and white images, allowing the drama and mood of the subject to shine. He prefers the look and atmosphere of soft light, often shooting on overcast or stormy days that keep other photographers at home.

Keith's photographs appeared in two major motion pictures including the 2016 movie Why Him? and the 2018 action film Peppermint, starring Jennifer Garner. His photographs had multi-season recurring roles on the sets of Grey's Anatomy and Melissa and Joey, and have appeared in other TV shows like Modern Family, Gossip Girl, The Mindy Project, and Tyler Perry's Sistas.

Andrés Rozo Samer



Andrés Rozo was born in Cali, Columbia in 1977 and is a Photographer and designer along his University professor career. He is also Italian by maternal inheritance and he currently resides in Columbia.
Andrés studied graphic design and specialised in multimedia creation and media arts; later, he persued a research-based master's degree in Design in order to reflect upon the role of photography in design, proposing the concept of electromagnetic graphs and photographic design. He has worked as a full-time professor at the Universidad Autónoma de Occidente in Cali for more than a decade.

Within England he actioned his quest for photographic and artistic excellence and has conducred portfolio reviews, obtaining 3 international accreditations. The first one was by the Federation of European Photographers (as a professional European photographer in the portrait category), the next with the British Institute of Professional Photography (in professional commercial photography) and the third as a fine art specialist obtained also with British Institute of Professional Photography. The British photographer François Dolmetsch has become his muse, focusing on the black and white technique.
